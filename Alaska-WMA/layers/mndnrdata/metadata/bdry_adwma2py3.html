Metadata Summary

Originator Minnesota DNR - Division of Fish & Wildlife
Abstract This polygon theme contains boundaries for approximately 1392 Wildlife Management Areas (WMAs) across the state covering nearly 1,288,000 acres. WMAs are part of the Minnesota state recreation system created to protect wildlife habitat and provide wildlife-based recreation.
Use Tips Due to the acquisition process, property boundary surveying and posting work, and access issues; some WMAs currently in the state recreation system may not appear in this boundary layer. The boundary layer should be accurate enough to provide information for management and planning activities as well as for general reference on maps. WMAs have restrictions on types of public use, therefore creating maps of WMAs as general recreation or hunting areas has some peril. Some portions of WMAs are wildlife sanctuaries, some portions are private land, some WMAs are only open to public use during certain times of the year, some require landowner permission for access and some WMAs are legally closed to the public. Contact with local Area Wildlife offices is important to avoid trespass issues. http://files.dnr.state.mn.us/contact/wildlife_managers.pdf

Originator Minnesota DNR - Division of Fish & Wildlife
Title State Wildlife Management Area Boundaries - Publicly Accessible
Abstract This polygon theme contains boundaries for approximately 1392 Wildlife Management Areas (WMAs) across the state covering nearly 1,288,000 acres. WMAs are part of the Minnesota state recreation system created to protect wildlife habitat and provide wildlife-based recreation.
Use Tips Due to the acquisition process, property boundary surveying and posting work, and access issues; some WMAs currently in the state recreation system may not appear in this boundary layer. The boundary layer should be accurate enough to provide information for management and planning activities as well as for general reference on maps. WMAs have restrictions on types of public use, therefore creating maps of WMAs as general recreation or hunting areas has some peril. Some portions of WMAs are wildlife sanctuaries, some portions are private land, some WMAs are only open to public use during certain times of the year, some require landowner permission for access and some WMAs are legally closed to the public. Contact with local Area Wildlife offices is important to avoid trespass issues. http://files.dnr.state.mn.us/contact/wildlife_managers.pdf
Purpose There are two primary purposes for the WMA boundary layer. Area staff will use it for managing WMAs; local mapping, contracting for maintenance and development projects, visitor use maps, management use maps, project planning and acquisition planning. The layer will also be used for public information about access to and activities on WMAs. WMA boundaries are included on the DNR web site, with visitor use information. These boundaries are for general reference. While they can be used in a GPS, they are not accurate enough to legally locate boundary lines. Avoid trespass onto private lands by observing posted boundary signs, or contacting the local Area Wildlife office for detailed information. http://files.dnr.state.mn.us/contact/wildlife_managers.pdf

Entity and Attribute Overview Data Elements for Attribute Table BDRY_ADWMAPY3.DBF:

COUNTY: Two digit county code

PGM_PRJ: Management Unit ID number

AREANAME: Area Office Name

ID: Unique polygon ID number

UNITNAME: WMA name

SUBUNIT: WMA sub-unit name

ACRES: Area of polygon in acres

SOURCE: Primary digitizing source

DATE_INV: Date inventoried

DIRECTIONS: Directions to "main" access

NEAREST_TO: City nearest to WMA

RESTRICT: Any special restrictions

DEER: Hunting opportunities for deer.

BEAR: Hunting opportunities for bear.

MOOSE: Hunting opportunities for moose.

SMGAME: Hunting opportunities for small game.

FSTBIRDS: Hunting opportunities for forest upland birds.

SHARPTAILS: Hunting opportunities for Sharptail grouse.

PHEASANTS: Hunting opportunities for pheasants/partridge.

WATERFOWL: Hunting opportunities for waterfowl.

TURKEY: Hunting opportunities for turkey.

WETNONGAME: Viewing opportunities for wetland non game.

FORNONGAME: Viewing opportunities for forest non game.

PRANONGAME: Viewing opportunities for prairie non game.

SPECOPPS: Special recreational opportunities

HANDICAP: Handicap opportunities

HEADER: Description of WMA

COMMENTS: Special comments from manager

WEBREADY: Ready for detailed web application.

PHOTO: Photo exists for web application.

FORMAT: Panoramic photo format

SUBUNITNUM: Subunit number

UNIQUEID: Prm_prj + SubUnitNum.AsString

ACCESSIBLE: Indicates if a WMA has an accessible facility on it. For example, if a wheelchair accessible hunting blind exists on a WMA it would have a ?Y? in this field.
